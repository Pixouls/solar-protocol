Solar Protocol

The first algorithm was made by the sun

Solar Protocol is an experimental network of solar powered servers installed at different locations around the world. As the sun rises and sets, each server turns on and off as its solar panel goes into sunlight or darkness. Traffic is directed to whichever server is enjoying the most sunlight at the time a request for the Solar Protocol website is made. Different parts of this site are distributed across the network and become available at different times of the day, depending on which server is active.

The system directs traffic using the Domain Network Service (DNS) protocol, a decentralized system that resolves a URL to an IP address of a server. In short, DNS dictates the path between client and server. Typically high volume web services like Google or Facebook will configure the DNS to direct network traffic to different servers based on their response times which is ususally a proxy for the sending traffic to the server that is geographically closest. However on the Solar Protocol network, traffic is automatically directed to whichever server has the most solar energy available. The distribution of sunshine (and therefore energy) across the planet detirmines the path from client to server.

Natural intelligences

Solar Protocol reconfigures internet protocols using natural rather than artificial intelligence. Presently, much attention is focused on automating decisions based on logics derived from massive datasets, produced by systems of surveillance capitalism. Web platforms monitor our our every click, view and scroll and then use this data to make decisions about what content to show us next. Instead, on the Solar Protocol platform, decisions about what content is served are automated with a logic produced by the sunâ€™s position. The network operates with a natural intelligence that emerges from the intermittent dynamics of our shared environment.

Both digital surveillance and machine learning are intelligences only made possible by a society dependent on cheap energy from fossil fuels. Relying on solar energy to power our servers, means the pages served on this network must be kept small in size and have to omit energy-intensive tracking technologies.1 Solar power therefore encourages a faster, lighter and more private internet than that powered by fossil fuels.

Solar Protocol is a space to explore the interplay between the social, ecological and technological, and imagine the internet anew.

1. This approach was used by the Solar Powered Website published by Low Tech Magazine (2019). We have taken much inspiration from this prior work.
